About Yogyakarta

The city of Yogyakarta (Javanese:ꦪꦺꦴꦒꦾꦏꦂꦠ, , translit. Ngayogyakarta, Javanese pronunciation: [kuʈɔ ajogjɔˈkart̪ɔ]) or known by the local community as the City of Jogja or the City of Yogya is the capital and administrative center of the province of the Special Region of Yogyakarta, Indonesia. This city is a big city that maintains traditional concepts and Javanese culture. The city of Yogyakarta became the residence of Sultan Hamengkubuwana and the Duke of Paku Alam. One of the sub-districts in Yogyakarta, namely Kotagede, was the center of the Sultanate of Mataram between the years 1575–1640. The palaces (palaces) that are still functioning in the true sense are the Ngayogyakarta Palace and Puro Paku Alaman, which are fragments of the Mataram Sultanate. During the revolution, Yogyakarta was also the capital of Indonesia between 1946 and 1950.

National Heroine

The following are some names of national heroes from Yogyakarta.

Ki Hajar Dewantoro
His first name is Raden Mas Soewardi Soeryaningrat and comes from the Yogyakarta royal family. Born on May 2, 1889 and died in Yogyakarta on April 28, 1959.
Kyai Haji Ahmad Dahlan
A leader of Muslims in Java and founder of Muhammadiyah on 18-11-1912. He comes from Yogyakarta, born in 1868 and died in 1923, with a small name Muhammad Darwis.
Pangeran Diponegoro
Another name is Bendoro Raden Mas Ontowiryo, born in Yogyakarta on November 11, 1785 and died in Makassar on 18-01-1855.

Best Mountain in the Yogyakarta

The name Merapi is a compound of Sanskrit Meru meaning "mountain" with Javanese api which means "fire". [citation needed] Thus Merapi can be loosely translated as "Mountain of Fire" or "Fire Mountain".

Mount Merapi

Mount Merapi, is an active stratovolcano located on the border between the province of Central Java and the Special Region of Yogyakarta, Indonesia. It is the most active volcano in Indonesia and has erupted regularly since 1548. It is located approximately 28 km (17 mi) north of Yogyakarta city which has a population of 2.4 million, and thousands of people live on the flanks of the volcano, with villages as high as 1,700 m (5,577 ft) above sea level.


Mount Merapi

Mount Merapi is the site of a very active volcano monitoring program. The Babadan , Selo , and Plawangan monitoring stations have been updated with equipment over the decades since establishment. Some of the pre-1930 observation posts were destroyed by the 1930 eruption, and newer posts were re-located.
